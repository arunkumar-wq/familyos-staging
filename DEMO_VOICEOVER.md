# LINIO — AI Voiceover Script

**Format:** Continuous spoken narration — no stage directions, no bullet points, no formatting marks.
**Ready for:** ElevenLabs, Murf.ai, Play.ht, WellSaid, or any text-to-speech tool.
**Duration:** ~4 minutes 20 seconds at natural speaking pace.
**Voice recommendation:** Male, professional, warm, mid-30s. Moderate pace. American English.

---

## FULL SCRIPT (copy-paste into AI voice tool)

Let me ask you something. Where is your family's passport right now? Your mortgage agreement? Your kids' vaccination records? Your life insurance policy?

For most families, critical documents are scattered. Some in a drawer. Some on a phone. Some buried in email. Some... nowhere at all.

The average American family manages over two hundred important documents. When one expires, when tax season hits, when something urgent comes up, people scramble. They miss deadlines. They pay penalties. They lose track of what they even have.

LINIO fixes that.

LINIO is an AI-powered family command center. It's one place where your family's documents, finances, and critical deadlines all live together, organized, tracked, and monitored automatically.

Let me show you how it works.

Here's the login screen. I'm signing in as Gurmail Singh, the head of a family of five.

And here's the dashboard. Your family's financial pulse, at a glance.

Net worth, two point eight five million dollars. Twenty-three documents on file. Five family members, all active. And right here, five AI-generated alerts that need attention.

This chart tracks net worth over the last twenty-four months. You can see the trend, the growth, the trajectory.

Now look at these alerts. These are not static reminders. The AI has scanned everything and flagged what actually matters. Passport expiring in forty-five days. Insurance review recommended. A missing document detected. Portfolio rebalance needed.

This is the kind of thing families miss until it's too late. LINIO catches it automatically.

And here's the whole family. Gurmail, Lovely, Raj, Priya, and Mom. Each with their own access level.

Now let's look at the document vault. Twenty-three documents, organized across twelve categories. Identity documents. Insurance policies. Financial and tax. Legal. Medical. Everything categorized automatically.

Every document shows its status. Current, expiring, or needs review. Plus an AI confidence score showing how well the system understood it.

Now watch this. I'm going to upload a document.

I just dropped a file. The AI is reading it right now.

In under two seconds, the AI detected this is a passport. It extracted the expiry date, identified the document type with ninety-seven percent confidence, and auto-assigned it to the right family member.

No manual entry. No tagging. No filing. The AI does it all. One click, and it's securely stored.

Here's the financial picture. Three point four million dollars in assets. Five hundred sixty-five thousand in liabilities. Net worth, two point eight five million.

Twenty-four months of net worth history. You can filter by six months, twelve months, or all time. Every account is here. Schwab brokerage, real estate, four-oh-one-k accounts, savings, even crypto. All in one view.

And the AI is watching your portfolio too. It's recommending rebalancing, flagging tax-loss harvesting opportunities, and alerting you that your emergency fund only covers two point eight months. The target is six.

LINIO is built for families, not individuals. Five family members. Each has a role. Admin, co-admin, member, or view-only. Each has granular permissions for what they can see and edit.

Gurmail and Lovely manage everything. The kids have limited access. Mom can view but not change anything. It's like having a family IT department.

Now, this is where LINIO gets really smart.

I'm running a full AI analysis right now. It's scanning all twenty-three documents, checking expiry dates, looking for missing coverage, analyzing the portfolio.

Done. Eleven findings. Eight critical items, one warning, two informational. The AI found that Gurmail's passport expires in forty-five days, three family members are missing key documents, and the emergency fund is below target.

This isn't a to-do list someone typed. This is AI reading your actual documents and finances and telling you what to do next.

Let me recap what makes LINIO different.

One. AI document detection. Upload anything, the AI reads it, categorizes it, and tracks its expiry.

Two. Family-first design. Five roles, granular permissions, multi-member support.

Three. Proactive alerts. The AI doesn't wait for you to ask. It scans, analyzes, and tells you what needs attention.

Four. Complete financial picture. Net worth, assets, liabilities, allocation, all tracked with twenty-four months of history.

Every family has hundreds of critical documents and financial accounts. Today, they're scattered across filing cabinets, email inboxes, and phone photos. Nobody has a complete picture until something goes wrong.

LINIO gives families that complete picture. Organized, monitored, and protected by AI.

One place. One family. Total clarity.

That's LINIO.
